Rules ralated act 1994

The primary legislation related to organ transplantation in India is the "Transplantation of Human Organs Act, 1994."

Here, now we can see some rules related organ donation act 1994.

  • Brain death certification

The Act defines the conditions and criteria for determining brain death, which is a legal prerequisite for organ donation. A medical board must certify brain death before organ retrieval can take place.

  • Conset

Consent for organ donation must be obtained from the donor or their family. Living donors must provide informed consent, and deceased donors should have expressed their consent before death or through their family members after death.

  • Activities

The Act prohibits the sale and purchase of organs. It also prohibits commercial dealings related to organ transplantation.

  • Authorization

Only authorized hospitals and medical professionals can perform organ transplantation procedures. They must be registered with the appropriate authority.

  • Allocation

Organs are allocated to patients based on medical criteria and urgency, as well as factors like blood type and tissue compatibility.

  • Penalties

The Act prescribes penalties for violations, including illegal organ trafficking, unauthorized transplants, and non-compliance with regulations.
