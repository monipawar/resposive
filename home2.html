Laws and Some rules related Organ Donation in India

Organ donation laws accentutaion the significance of the donor's or their legal representative's informed permission. laws are typically describe minimum age and mental capacity which is legally agree to organ donation. Laws established some protocol living or non-living doner we follow all the rules when we donate organs. The surgical methods, transplantation teams, transplant facilities, post-transplant care guidelines, and reporting requirements are all governed by laws and regulations. The Primary provision related to Organ donation and Transplantation in India. Transplantation of Human Organs had Passed in 1994 is aimed that regulation of expulsion, storage and Transplantation of Human Organs for prevention of commercial dealing in Humans organs.In India matter related to health are governed by each state. Organs Donation is the legal Process which is done legally. The law allow us both defunct and living doner to donate their Organs. India has performed the second largest number of transplant in the world in 2019.

Some rules of the Act which are follow from 2014.

(a). Process and Criteria for brain death Certifictaion. Brain death identify as a form of death.

(b). Allow Transplantation of Organ and Tissue form Living Doner and cadavers(after cardiac or brain death)

(c). Living donors are classified as either a near relative or a non-related donor. A near-relative (spouse, children, grandchildren, siblings, parents and grandparents) needs permission of the doctor in-charge of the transplant center to donate his organ. A non-related donor needs permission of an Authorization Committee established by the state to donate his organs.

(d). When a near relative living doner is madically incompaitible with the reciever. The pair is permitted to do a swap transplant with another related unmatched donor/recipient pair.

(e). Cost of donor management, retrieval, transportation and preservation to be borne by the recipient, institution, government, NGO or society, and not by the donor family.

(f). Procedure for organ donation in medico-legal cases defined to avoid jeopardizing determination of the cause of death and delay in retrieval of organs.
