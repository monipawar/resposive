About Liver and Heart

Liver

Donating a liver, either as a living donor or after passing away, can have a profound impact on the recipient and their quality of life. Liver donation can save the life of a person suffering from severe liver disease or failure. Without a transplant, many of these individuals would not survive.For recipients, a successful liver transplant often results in a significantly improved quality of life. It allows them to resume normal activities and enjoy a better standard of living. Liver transplantation can lead to long-term survival for the recipient. It is considered a life-saving intervention for patients with advanced liver disease.liver transplantation is often the only effective treatment for certain liver diseases, including cirrhosis, liver cancer, acute liver failure, and specific genetic liver conditions.After a successful liver transplant, recipients can undergo rehabilitation and regain their strength, allowing them to return to a relatively normal lifestyle.

Heart

Heart donation is a life-saving gift. Transplantation offers a new chance at life for individuals suffering from end-stage heart failure, providing them with an opportunity to lead longer, healthier lives.A successful heart transplant significantly improves the quality of life for the recipient. It allows them to regain normal heart functioning, engage in physical activities, and enjoy an improved overall sense of well-being. Heart transplantation often results in long-term survival for recipients, providing them with years of life they might not have had otherwise.Heart failure often comes with severe symptoms like shortness of breath, fatigue, chest pain, and limited physical activity. A heart transplant can alleviate these symptoms, allowing the recipient to enjoy a more active and fulfilling life.
