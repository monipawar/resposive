History of organ donation

In modern time organ tranplant began to develop in 20th century.There are historical reports of organ replacement and transplantation in many different societies, frequently tied to mythology and religious beliefs. These stories, meanwhile, lacked actual surgical operations and were more metaphorical in nature. Some experimental made in 1990s which was related skin and cornea but it was not successful because lack of knowledge and understanding. The ability to suppress the immune system and lower the risk of transplant rejection was made feasible by the invention of immunosuppressive medications (such as corticosteroids) in the 1950s. The first successful kidney transplant was performed by Dr. Joseph Murray and Dr. David Hume in Boston, USA in 1954 the doner and recipient both was twins and risk of rejection was minimal.

In 1960s-1970s Organ transplantation, particularly kidney transplants, became more common and successful as advancements in surgical techniques and immunosuppressive medications continued to evolve. In 1970s-1980s Organ procurement organizations (OPOs) were established to coordinate organ donations and increase the efficiency of organ allocation and distribution. In 1987 The United Network for Organ Sharing (UNOS) was established in the United States to maintain a national registry for organ matching and allocation.Throughout the latter half of the 20th century and into the 21st century, various countries developed legislation and ethical guidelines to regulate organ donation and transplantation, ensuring the safety, fairness, and ethical treatment of donors and recipients.

Living donor transplants, where organs or parts of organs are donated by living individuals, gained popularity and success in the late 20th century and continue to be an essential part of organ transplantation. Advancements in surgical techniques, immunosuppressive drugs, tissue typing, and understanding of immunology have significantly improved the success rates and safety of organ transplantation and currently organ donation process is done very easily. doner can donate organs very easily give the second chance of life to the recipient.
