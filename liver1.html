Liver

The liver helps regulate blood glucose levels by converting excess glucose into glycogen for storage or breaking down glycogen into glucose when blood sugar levels drop. It processes and metabolizes fats, including the synthesis of cholesterol and triglycerides and the breakdown of fatty acids.The liver is involved in synthesizing and breaking down proteins, including the production of important proteins like albumin and clotting factors.The liver filters and detoxifies harmful substances, including drugs and toxins, by breaking them down and excreting them from the body.

The liver produces bile, a substance that aids in the digestion and absorption of fats in the digestive system. Bile is stored in the gallbladder and released into the small intestine.The liver produces proteins necessary for blood clotting, preventing excessive bleeding.The liver helps regulate hormone levels by metabolizing and breaking down hormones like insulin, estrogen, and thyroid hormones.The liver plays a central role in metabolism, including the processing of nutrients from the food we eat. It regulates blood sugar levels by storing and releasing glucose as needed, and it helps metabolize fats and proteins.
