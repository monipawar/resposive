Act

The Transplantation of Human Organs Act, 1994 is an important legislation in India that regulates the removal, storage, and transplantation of human organs for therapeutic purposes. The Act aims to govern organ transplantation, prevent commercial dealings in organs, and ensure ethical practices and transparency in the process of organ donation and transplantation. The main objective of the Act is to regulate the removal, storage, and transplantation of human organs and tissues for therapeutic purposes and to prevent commercial dealings in organs.

The Act provides definitions for terms related to organ transplantation, brain death, registered medical practitioners, relatives, and more. The Act emphasizes the need for informed consent from the donor or the authorized person for the removal of organs. Consent must be voluntary and without any coercion.The Act establishes authorization committees at the national and state levels to oversee and grant approval for organ transplants.
