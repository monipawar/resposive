Tissue Donation

Tissue donation involves the voluntary donation of various tissues from a deceased individual (non-living donor) to help improve the lives of others. tissue donation focuses on donating non-vital tissues that can greatly benefit recipients in need. Tissue transplantation can enhance a person's quality of life, aid in healing, and provide essential support for a variety of medical conditions and injuries.

Types of Tissue organs

  • Cornea
  • Bones
  • Skins
  • Heart Valves
  • Ligaments
  • Cornea

The cornea is the clear, front part of the eye that covers the iris and pupil. Donated corneas can be used to restore vision for individuals with corneal diseases, injuries, or other vision-related issues.

  • Bones

Donated bone tissue can be used in orthopedic surgeries to help repair fractures, fill voids, or provide structural support in bone reconstruction procedures.

  • Skins

Donated skin tissue can be used for grafts in burn patients or those with severe skin loss due to trauma, infection, or certain medical conditions. It helps protect against infection and promote healing.

  • Heart Valves

Heart valves from donors can be used to replace damaged heart valves in individuals with heart conditions, improving cardiac function and circulation.

  • Tendons and Ligaments

Donated tendons and ligaments can be used to repair or reconstruct damaged tendons and ligaments, often seen in sports injuries or trauma.
